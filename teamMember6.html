Prof. Bonolo Ramadi Dinokopila, Consultant
(University of Botswana), LLM, LLD (University of Pretoria)

Professor Dinokopila graduated from the University of Botswana in 2007. He read for and completed his Master’s Degree (cum laude) in Human Rights and Democratization in Africa (H.R.D.A) at the University of Pretoria in 2008. He obtained his Doctor of Laws (LLD) degree in 2013 at the same University specialising in international human rights law, public international law and international institutional law. Upon completion of his LLB studies in 2007, Prof. Dinokopila joined Makuyana Legal Practice from June 2006-August 2007. He then joined the University of Botswana as a teaching assistant from September 2007 to December 2007. He worked for the Botswana Network on Ethics Law & HIV/AIDS from June 2009 to September 2009. He then joined Duma Boko & Company as an Associate Attorney from October 2009 to November 2012.

From December 2012 to June 2013 he was the legal consultant for Morolong Legal Practice. During his time as a legal practitioner Prof. Dinokopila’s focus was on general civil litigation, labour law issues (including appearances before the Botswana Defence Force and Botswana Police Service disciplinary boards), family law, constitutional law and Administrative Law. From April 2010 to June 2013 he worked as a lecturer and later as a senior lecturer at the University of Botswana during which time he attended international conferences and published widely on international human rights and constitutional law. He joined the Administration of Justice in July 2013 as the Assistant Registrar & Master of the High Court subsequently joined Dinokopila Lekgowe and Lerumo Mogobe Legal Practitioners as a Partner positions he held until he was appointed Head of the Department of Law, University of Botswana in 2016 and in January 2020 respectively.

Prof. Dinokopila has done consultancy work for the International Labour Organization (ILO), the Disability Office in the Office of the President in Collaboration with UNFPA, International Law Enforcement Academy (ILEA) a collaborative effort between the governments of the United States of America (USA) and Botswana as well as the Botswana Network on Ethics, Law & HIV/AIDS (BONELA). He is also the Chairperson of the Board of Directors, BONELA. He was, until December 2019, the Acting Chairperson of the Country Advisory Committee (CAC), Southern African Reproductive and Health Rights Trust (SAT) and was Vice Chair, Rainbow Identity Association in 2011.
